About Me

I am a graduate student at the University of Southern California pursuing Masters in Computer Science.

I worked at the U.S. Army Research Lab for the summer of 2020, helping them build predictive models to tell the attention state of subjects.
During the summer of 2018, I worked at the Computer Vision Lab, at Indiana University Bloomington on a system that can classify notes played and finger used by a piano player using the video and audio feeds. Now I am working on preserving privacy of non-subjects in photographs using GANs.

Experience

U.S. Army CCDC

Software Engineer Intern

I worked at the Combat Capabilities Development Command of the U.S. Army Research Lab to design and implement an algorithm to determine if a subject is currently looking at predefined target from the changes to their pupil size. I helped prepare the dataset for this task and designed the algorithm. My algorithm uses deep neural networks to classify from a 5 second window of pupil sizes whether the subject is looking at a target or not with a 94% accuracy on a test set.

Indiana University Bloomington

Software Engineer Intern

I worked at the Computer Vision Lab of Indiana University Bloomington to develop a Piano tutor that records the subject playing the piano and responds to incorrect notes played and fingering used for predetermined pieces. Developed a neural network architecture that determines note played and finger used from an audio and visual stream of the subject's hands on the piano. Uses hand landmarks detected from OpenPose.

Precog Research Group

Software Developer

Public portal developed to visualize data gathered and answered questions posed by journalists such as tracking growth of specific hashtags, accounts that are active only during the election cycle, accounts that were deleted.

Ultimate Tic Tac Toe

I participated and was a semi-finalist in a competition to create a bot to play ultimate TicTacToe (4x4x4x4) with 30 seconds given per move. Used the minimax algorithm with alpha beta pruning to find the best move in the game tree. Best move determined with heuristic score from features of the board at max depth. Flexible maximum depth obtained by iterative deepening.
